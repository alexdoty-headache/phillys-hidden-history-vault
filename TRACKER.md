### Washing up / ablution as hospitality (B-125 · B-162 · B-150)

**Flagged:** 2026-07-21
**Action needed:** None tonight — candidate theme for future tour script development.

After a long, hot, dusty train journey, the opportunity to wash before eating was experienced as a genuine luxury. Three entries document this:

- **B-125** (`anec-b125`) — 1st Maine Heavy Artillery: *"the refreshing bath that many had"* is named explicitly alongside the meal as a highlight of the Cooper Shop stop. Notable tier.
- **B-162** (`anec-b162`, **standout**) — 38th Massachusetts: *"conducted to a long row of wash-basins provided for the purpose. After the hot and dusty ride, the ablution was peculiarly refreshing, and was greatly enjoyed."* The most vivid account; pull quote wired into the Vault.
- **B-150** (`anec-b150`, **standout**) — 7th Connecticut, Walkley 1905: foundational account of Cooper Shop origins; thematically adjacent to the ablution theme.

**Tour script angle:** The saloons didn't just feed soldiers — they restored basic human dignity (clean hands, fresh water) before the meal. This is a subtle but distinctive detail that differentiates these accounts from generic Civil War hospitality stories. Works well as a transition beat in a Cooper Shop segment.
