Alex Doty, Founder & Chief Storyteller

My 25 years in urban policy, transportation, and civic leadership provided a deep, structural lens through which to view history. My passion is telling stories with a purpose, and I founded Philly's Hidden History Vault to explore history through the lens of strategic dissection.

I believe history is compellingly complex. Guides who simplify history lose their audience; I embrace the hidden stories and intriguing turns history offers. I specialize in performing the strategic dissection of historical outcomes, moving beyond hero narratives to explore the logistical, financial, and political forces that shape our world.

I've found that the best way to process complexity is on the move — I think better when I'm walking, and I know you will, too.

A Personal Connection to Philadelphia's Past

My roots run deep here. My ancestors have lived in Philadelphia since before the Revolution. I am proud to include them in my tours, notably as the great-great-grandson of the artist behind The Morgan Dollar, giving me a unique, personal lens through which to tell the story of the immigrants and institutions who defined the American identity.

My Focus: Policy, Logistics, and Civic Resilience

In 2026, as Philadelphia hosts the world for the 250th Anniversary, my tours offer essential context. My expertise is utilized to:

  • Strategically dissect the Confederate invasion, proving it was doomed by infrastructure failure.
  • Analyze the logistics & urban scale that defined the ultimate test of American power.
  • Examine the civic mobilization and financial decisions that secured the Union's victory.

These are not traditional walking tours; they are urban seminars on American history.

Some Things I Love

  • Performing the strategic dissection of historical supply chains and institutional mobilization (from D-Day to the Civil War).
  • Historic cemeteries (especially those in West Philadelphia) and walking neighborhood streets on my travels.
  • Walking the ground at Gettysburg to reflect on the human cost of war.
  • The Phanatic!

The Vault: Where the Dissection Comes From

Every claim on a tour has to earn its place, which is why the same rigor runs behind the scenes. The Vault is the primary-source archive underneath it — ledger entries, committee rosters, and firsthand accounts from the institutions that actually moved men, money, and supplies through Civil War Philadelphia. It's built to be explored on its own, whether or not you ever take a tour.
