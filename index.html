The History You Thought You Knew Deserves a Closer Look

You've heard of the "Highwater Mark of the Confederacy" at Gettysburg. But what if General Lee had actually won the battle? Conventional history suggests a victory would have opened the door to Confederate independence. Join Philly's Hidden History Vault as we challenge that myth on this immersive Civil War walking tour of Philadelphia.

While other tours recite the same stories, we scrutinize the hidden history in plain sight. This is a 2.5-hour intellectual investigation designed for serious history buffs and those looking for educational things to do in Philadelphia that challenge the textbooks.

On This In-Depth Tour, You Will Discover:

  • The Logistical Nightmare — why conquering a city the size of Philadelphia, with the Army of Northern Virginia's notoriously fragile supply lines, was an impossible military task.
  • The Political Paradox — why a Confederate victory might have been the best thing for Abraham Lincoln.
  • Lee's Fatal Flaw — the systematic policy of re-enslaving free Black citizens on Northern soil, and how it guaranteed European non-recognition.
  • The Full-Spectrum Power — the Union's 11.5:1 manufacturing advantage and superior demographics.

Tour Itinerary & Highlights

  • City Hall (McClellan Statue): the Union's command structure and Philadelphia's defenses.
  • The Union League: the financial engine of the Union war effort.
  • Pennsylvania Hospital: the "Medical Crisis of 1863."
  • Washington Square: the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier.
  • Independence Hall: the ultimate stakes — if Philadelphia falls, does the Union fall with it?
